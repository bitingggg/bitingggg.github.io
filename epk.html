If you're always big, you're always small.

Biting is an alternative rock band that challenges the impending maximalism of today's soundscape. Abstaining from walls of overdubbed guitars, artifically-enhanced drum parts, and pitch-corrected vocals, they instead choose to deliver something that's getting increasingly hard to find: authenticity.

In fact, there's not a single silver spoon to be found throughout the lineup—instead, its members have cut their teeth as session musicians, playing with the likes of Men I Trust, King Princess, and Still Woozy.

But, of course, you can't just remain a hired gun forever. At some point, you've got to throw it all away just to see if you can do it all over again.

Biting is that gambit—and it can be heard in the music. It's four dyed-in-the-wool musicians walking away from safety and giving it their all. It's beautiful, raw catalog that rides the fine line between grace and chaos. They aren't afraid to strip everything down to just a voice and a bass guitar—making that full band entrance on the downbeat of the chorus feel that much harder.

It's this Pixies-like dynamicism that fills their songs with musical peaks and valleys. And it isn't just restricted to their recorded material, either. The band notoriously rehearses in pitch black so that when they're playing live, they're not sheepishly looking at their fretboards or drumsticks... they're looking at you and making sure they give you everything they've got.

It's hard to leave home. But sometimes, if you just allow yourself to be small—it'll give you the opportunity to become one of the biggest things anyone's ever seen.
